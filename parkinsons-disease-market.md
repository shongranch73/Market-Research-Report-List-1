<p><p>The Parkinson's disease market comprises several drug categories. Carbidopa/Levodopa is the primary treatment, converting into dopamine in the brain to alleviate symptoms. Dopamine receptor agonists stimulate dopamine receptors directly, offering an alternative or adjunct therapy. MAO-inhibitors block the breakdown of dopamine, increasing its availability and prolonging effects. Each class targets different aspects of Parkinson's, catering to varying stages and patient responses, ultimately enhancing the overall management of the disease and improving patients' quality of life.</p></p>
